Message from the Head of the Department

Who should choose Data Engineering?

We take great pride in introducing the recently established Data Engineering Department within the School of Computer Science. This department is dedicated to three distinct sections, which have been designed and categorized based on emerging technologies in the field of Computer Science.

These undergraduate engineering courses have been started by the institute from the academic year 2022-23 with the intake capacity of 60 seats per section.

The sections include:
1. Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning
2. Data Science
3. Internet of Things and Cybersecurity, including Blockchain Technology

Our programs under the Department of Data Engineering aim to excel as an educational center for technical education. Our goal is to transform aspiring students into skilled and well-rounded software professionals. We emphasize a strong understanding of fundamentals, a flair for responsible innovation in engineering practical and robust solutions, and instill confidence to meet the challenges in their chosen professional domains.

Those who enjoy problem-solving, have strong analytical skills, and possess a desire to contribute to the technological advancements of various domains would find Data Engineering an ideal choice.

What are the opportunities in Data engineering?

These emerging technologies have become integral to the engineering field, bringing forth significant advancements and opportunities across various domains such as healthcare, robotics, prediction, decision-making, cybersecurity, and automation. It is essential to consider data quality, model accuracy, interpretability, and ethical aspects in these domains.

How is the current industry scenario and how MVGR designed its courses to tackle these industry challenges?

These programs offer a comprehensive curriculum that meets current industry requirements in relevant fields. Project-based learning is an integral part of our curriculum, providing students with hands-on experience. We also provide students with awareness of various career-oriented certifications and guidance to pursue them. To expose students to new challenges and cutting-edge technology, we invite industry experts for guest lectures, real data analysis projects, seminar presentations, and potential internships.

Other unique aspects of DE at MVGR

Students graduating with a degree in Computer Science - Data Engineering will not only be well-equipped to build successful careers in the industry and contribute to the entrepreneurial landscape of India but also have the option to pursue higher studies in their field of interest.

Overall, these programs offer a curriculum that fosters the overall development of students, equipping them with relevant technologies and the ability to apply them responsibly while aligning with regulatory standards to ensure safety, reliability, and societal well-being.

Curiculum

The Choice Based Credit System allows students to move to Institutions across the world with flexibility to transfer their credits.The proposed CBCS allows the students to choose from core electives, free electives and audit courses. 29 elective choices are provided out of which a student has to choose any 8 elective courses that he/she would pursue in various semesters starting from 1 elective in 5th Semester, 1 elective in 6th Semester, 5 electives in 7th Semester and 1 elective (a self-study course with optional attendance requirement) in 8th Semester.

The electives are divided into 3 thrust areas, Business Intelligence, Networks, Enterprise Architecture and 1 Generic group, with each of the thrust areas having 7 electives and generic group having 8 options. Any student should choose 1 of the 3 thrust areas in which he/she will do 4 electives thereby indicating that thrust area as his/her major choice. The thrust area has Level 2 courses four of which are advanced, are designed to cater to Industry standards and are dependent on two Level 1 courses. One Level two course has no dependency.Any student should also mandatorily choose 2 electives from the generic group. He can go to a maximum of 3.

He picks 1 of these 2 groups as his/her minor group and picks 1 Level 3 or 1 level 3 and 1 level 4 from that group. This way, the course structure has been designed to allow complete flexibility to students while at the same time, preserving dependencies between the core (prerequisite) courses and the advanced(Research/Industry oriented) courses.
